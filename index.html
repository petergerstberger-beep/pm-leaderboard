PM Operating Playbook
Your complete guide to operating as a PM at Jiffy. Every initiative flows through 5 stages. Your value = structuring ambiguity + feeding it to AI. This playbook shows you exactly how to do that at each stage, what tools to use, and how each stage maps to your score on the Dashboard.

1
INTAKE — Build & Maintain a Healthy Pipeline
Feeds pillars: Pipeline Health (20%) + Strategic Alignment (30%)
▼
WHY THIS STAGE EXISTS
A PM without a pipeline is a PM without leverage. Leadership drops strategic priorities; your job is to catch them, tag them, and keep the funnel full. If your backlog runs dry, engineering starves and your Pipeline Health score tanks. If your items don’t map to Kevin’s chip KPIs, your Strategic Alignment score tanks. This stage is the foundation — get it wrong and nothing downstream matters.
SKILLS & TOOLS TO USE
  • Jira — Log every item as an epic or story with priority, chip tag, and strategic alignment label
  • Dashboard → Business Directives — Cross-reference every intake item against Kevin’s 6 chips. If it doesn’t map, flag it
  • Weekly 1:1 with Pete — Review pipeline depth, surface items that need leadership input on prioritization
WHAT YOU PRODUCE
  • A Jira backlog with ≥4 weeks of work at current throughput
  • Every item tagged: priority level, chip assignment, business case (1-liner minimum)
  • Orphan report: items that don’t map to any chip KPI (escalate or kill)
  • RED FLAG: <2 weeks backlog = “LOW PIPELINE” — escalate immediately
  • RED FLAG: Items sitting in Intake >14 days without movement
MATURITY MODEL — Where Are You?
BEGINNER (Week 1–2)
Inheriting existing backlog. Learning chip assignments. Logging items but strategic tagging is inconsistent. Relies on manager to prioritize.
INTERMEDIATE (Month 1–2)
Proactively maintaining 4+ weeks of pipeline depth. Every item tagged to a chip. Flagging orphans and stale items before they’re caught in review. Starting to generate own intake from customer signals.
EXPERT (Month 3+)
Pipeline is self-sustaining. PM is generating strategic initiatives from data (Redshift, claims, CST tickets). Pipeline depth never dips below 6 weeks. Items are pre-prioritized before leadership review. Engineering never starves.

STRUCTURE — The PM’s Core Value: Structuring Ambiguity
Feeds pillars: Strategic Alignment (30%) + PRD Quality (25%) + AI Leverage (15%)
▼
WHY THIS STAGE EXISTS
This is where you earn your keep. Anyone can log a ticket. The PM’s unique value is taking an ambiguous business problem and defining the context, constraints, edge cases, and decisions that an AI can’t figure out on its own. The AI is the engine, but you’re the one defining the road. If you skip this step and go straight to “writing tickets,” you’re doing zero-value work.
SKILLS & TOOLS TO USE
  • Exec Readiness Prompt (MANDATORY) — Run every initiative through it first. It generates a structured problem definition, identifies gaps, and forces you to articulate what the AI needs from you
  • Claude — AI Sessions — Use Claude to brainstorm constraints, edge cases, alternative approaches. Every session counts toward your AI Leverage score
  • Decision Audit Trail Template — Start the log here. Document what you decided, why, and what you rejected (template below)
  • Dashboard → Business Directives — Explicitly reference which chip KPI your initiative serves in the problem statement
WHAT YOU PRODUCE
  • Exec Prompt Review score ≥ 7.0/10
  • Problem statement that captures what the AI can’t resolve alone
  • Decision log started with ≥3 key decisions documented
  • Constraints and edge cases enumerated (≥5 each)
  • Strategic alignment explicitly stated with chip KPI reference
  • ANTI-PATTERN: “Just writing tickets” without strategic context = zero value
  • ANTI-PATTERN: Lots of activity with nothing of measurable impact delivered
DECISION AUDIT TRAIL TEMPLATE — Start This at Structure, Complete at Output
## Problem Statement
What ambiguity existed? What couldn’t the AI figure out alone?

## Key Decisions Made
Decision 1: [what] — [reasoning] — [alternatives considered]
Decision 2: ...

## What Was Rejected & Why
Option A: [rejected because...]

## Assumptions to Validate Post-Launch
Assumption 1: [what needs to be true for this to work]

INTERMEDIATE (Month 1–2)
Exec Prompt scores consistently ≥7.0. Problem statements identify genuine ambiguity. Decision log has 3–5 entries with real reasoning. Using AI to brainstorm edge cases and stress-test assumptions. Can articulate chip KPI connection without prompting.
EXPERT (Month 3+)
Exec Prompt scores ≥8.5. Problem framing is so tight that GStack reviews have minimal rework. AI sessions are sophisticated multi-turn dialogues producing novel insights. Decision logs read like strategic memos. Leadership cites your problem framing in their own planning.

HARDEN — GStack Review Pipeline
Feeds pillars: PRD Quality (25%) + AI Leverage (15%)
▼
WHY THIS STAGE EXISTS
Structure gives you a draft. Hardening makes it bulletproof. The GStack skills are adversarial reviewers — they challenge your premises, stress-test your architecture, and find the gaps you missed. Each pass makes the PRD sharper. Diminishing returns kick in after ~4 passes, so aim for 3–4. PMs who skip hardening produce PRDs that engineering rejects or that require mid-sprint rewrites. That kills your PRD Quality score and wastes engineering capacity.
SKILLS & TOOLS TO USE (in order)
  • /office-hours — First pass. Structured problem statement review + premise challenge. Run this immediately after completing Structure
  • /plan-ceo-review — Second pass. Strategy and scope validation. Adversarial review from CEO perspective. Catches strategic misalignment early
  • /plan-design-review — Third pass (if UX-involved). UX review, accessibility, user flow validation. Generate prototypes/mockups via AI here to reduce UX dependency (~50% time savings)
  • /plan-eng-review — Final pass. Architecture and technical feasibility. Catches implementation blockers before handoff
  • Claude — Prototype Generation — Use Claude to generate wireframes, mockups, and flow diagrams. Every AI session counts toward your AI Leverage score
  • Decision Audit Trail — Update after each GStack pass with decisions made and feedback addressed
WHAT YOU PRODUCE
  • ≥3 GStack passes completed (office-hours + CEO review minimum)
  • At least 1 prototype/mockup for UX-involved work
  • Decision log updated with reasoning from each review pass
  • All premise challenges addressed (not just acknowledged)
  • No open P0 items remaining after final GStack pass
  • RED FLAG: PRD enters Output with 0 GStack passes = capped at 60
  • RED FLAG: PRD in “Needs Rework” >7 days = PRD Quality capped at 70
MATURITY MODEL — Where Are You?
BEGINNER (Week 1–2)
Running GStack skills but treating feedback as FYI rather than action items. 1–2 passes max. No prototypes generated. Decision trail not updated between passes. PRDs still get rejected by engineering.
INTERMEDIATE (Month 1–2)
Consistently hitting 3 GStack passes. Addressing all feedback items between passes. Generating at least 1 prototype per UX-involved PRD. Decision log tracks what changed and why after each review. Eng rejection rate dropping.
EXPERT (Month 3+)
PRDs emerge from hardening nearly production-ready. GStack feedback is minor refinement, not structural rework. Prototypes are high-fidelity enough that UX team approves with minimal changes. Decision trail is so thorough that leadership uses it as a strategic reference. Eng accepts on first pass >85% of the time.

OUTPUT — Ship the Hardened PRD
Feeds pillars: PRD Quality (25%) + Velocity (10%)
▼
WHY THIS STAGE EXISTS
This is your deliverable. The hardened PRD with SP estimates, a complete decision log, and crystal-clear acceptance criteria is what engineering consumes. Think of Output as your product — engineering is your customer. If they can’t start building within 48 hours of receiving it, you failed at this stage. Velocity matters here too: throughput of hardened PRDs is how the team scales to meet engineering capacity.
SKILLS & TOOLS TO USE
  • SP Estimator Skill — Run every PRD through the estimator for consistent Fibonacci sizing (1–13). Your estimate vs. engineering’s actual is your SP Inflation Index
